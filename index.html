Indoor-use shares and the measured per-use averages come from the Water Research Foundation's Residential End Uses of Water, Version 2 (2016) — the most rigorous U.S. household metering study — with efficient/standard fixture rates from EPA WaterSense and ENERGY STAR. That study measured indoor use at 52.1 gallons per person per day, below the older 80–100 estimate as efficient fixtures spread.

Step outside

Outdoors, the meter spins

Indoors you count gallons; outdoors you count hundreds. Nearly a third of a U.S. household's water is used outside — and up to half of it never reaches the plant.
